Dataout Ontology: Description back to ToC

This ontology is intended to represent data on SOGIESC-based crimes and discrimination, as well as processes associated with the collection of such data. The idea behind the Dataout Ontology is to represent instances of human rights violations as phenomena that may be observed in different sources, such as court cases or mass-media publications. There are two main components: the provenance information of an incident and its content. Each observed incident is modelled as an instance of Monitoring Record, resulting from Monitoring Activity. A monitoring record connects to the content and metadata of an incident. There are also controlled vocabularies intended for categorising instances. This ontology introduces custom classes and properties and reuses the PROV Ontology, Schema.org, and DCMI Metadata Terms.

Trigger Objectc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://dataout.org/ontology#TriggerObject

A trigger object is an information object or public action that was used by the authorities as a reason to initiate an administrative or criminal proceeding. For example, in our monitoring of administrative persecution targeting LGBTQIA+ expression in Russia, a trigger object could be a mass media publication, which the Russian mass media regulator (Roskomnadzor) found to be violating, which resulted in a court case against a media company dissimianting such publication.

is in range of
